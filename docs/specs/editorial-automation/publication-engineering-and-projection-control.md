## Purpose

Define the engineering invariants that keep editorial truth, publication effects and derived retrieval data reconstructable across crashes, retries, partial target failure, correction and projection outage.

## Scope

This specification covers authoritative records, governed-object identity, proposal admission, temporal semantics, projection replay, bounded retrieval, orthogonal publication state, immutable surface payloads, transactional dispatch, target acknowledgements, claim-to-surface traceability, reconciliation, local-ledger operation and degraded operation.

Capitalised domain terms have the meanings recorded in [`../../../CONTEXT.md`](../../../CONTEXT.md). PR 75 names are donor aliases only. The target system adopts this canonical vocabulary in its canonical production schema v1; normal governed schema evolution remains permitted, but no long-lived shadow compatibility model is required unless the owner separately approves a legacy-data import.

## Requirements

### Authority and derived projections

**ARCH-001 — Scoped authority.** The system MUST distinguish:

- governed content-addressed objects as authority for their exact retained bytes and content hashes, not for the truth of source claims;
- the editorial ledger as authority for the Newsroom's record of identities, observations, proposals, admissions, decisions, versions, publication bundles, target operations, attempts, received target responses, independently controlled target observations, reconciliation findings, remediation decisions and audit sequence;
- the publication controller as the sole executor permitted to create public effects from committed target operations; and
- graph, vector, full-text and online serving stores as derived projections or replicas unless a later accepted ADR explicitly changes that boundary.

The durable editorial ledger MUST be distinct from a short-lived discovery or candidate pool.

Authority here means authority for the Newsroom's system record. It does not make an admitted claim objectively true and does not override the actual externally observed state of a target. Target responses and observations remain untrusted evidence until validated and recorded.

**ARCH-002 — Persisted proposal admission.** A model-extracted entity mention, claim assertion or relation assertion MUST be persisted through the applicable immutable proposal with its exact provenance before a separate immutable admission decision. A relation or claim that depends on an entity mention MUST NOT be admitted until an effective entity-resolution decision binds that mention to an existing canonical entity or atomically creates and binds a new one. A rejected proposal MUST remain auditable subject to retention policy. Governed Claim and Governed Relation identities MUST be deterministic views of their proposal and effective decision identities; an extractor MUST NOT write them or the governed graph directly.

**ARCH-003 — Trust separation.** Proposal exploration and governed retrieval MUST use distinct, explicit trust scopes. Model confidence MUST NOT be interpreted as admission. An observed source statement or Governed Relation MUST NOT enter a publication bundle unless the applicable evidence, rights, risk and policy gates independently permit that use.

**ARCH-004 — Temporal semantics.** The system MUST distinguish at least:

- source publication and revision time;
- source retrieval and revision-observation time;
- asserted real-world validity start and end time;
- ledger recording time;
- entity, claim and relation admission and admission-revocation time;
- `primary_feed_published_at` and `first_public_effect_at`; and
- per-attempt `target_acknowledged_at`.

A query or payload MUST NOT collapse these meanings into one ambiguous `timestamp` or `as_of` value.

**ARCH-005 — Replayable projection.** Every governed projection mutation MUST be reproducible from ordered authoritative records by a versioned, idempotent projector. A rebuild under the same projector contract MUST replay retained extraction output and admission decisions or start from an Authoritative Projection Baseline and then apply later events and tombstones. It MUST NOT rerun a stochastic extractor and treat the new output as historical truth. A deliberate re-extraction under a new contract MUST create new proposals and decisions without mutating historical records.

**ARCH-006 — Contiguous projection watermark.** Every governed retrieval result MUST identify the contiguous ledger sequence and recording time through which all required events have been applied, together with projector and ontology versions, query-valid time and serving time. A later applied event MUST NOT conceal an earlier gap, dead letter or failed dependency.

**ARCH-007 — Projection retention.** A rights-expiry, privacy-deletion, legal-removal or retention decision MUST define its deletion scope and propagate to every covered graph, vector, full-text, cache and serving derivative. Rebuild logic MUST prevent prohibited data from being resurrected while preserving any audit or tombstone evidence that the decision permits.

**ARCH-008 — Durable object reference.** A committed ledger reference MUST never point to an incomplete or missing governed object, including a source capture, surface payload, asset or retained audit artefact. The system MUST atomically install each immutable object on the local supported filesystem, complete the configured durable flush and hash-verify the installed bytes before committing its ledger reference. Unreferenced objects MUST remain safely collectable. Off-machine replication is a separate backup operation and MUST NOT be described as part of the local ledger transaction.

**ARCH-009 — One canonical contract from inception.** Canonical production schema v1 MUST define stable identities, the ordered ledger-event envelope and the records required by publication, online serving and knowledge-graph projection. Publication, serving, governed-knowledge and operations projectors MUST share the same canonical identifiers and ordered ledger-event sequence from their first implementation. The publication controller MUST instead consume only committed `TargetOperation` records linked to the same canonical identifiers and originating ledger sequence; a `LedgerEvent` alone MUST NOT authorise a public side effect. Normal versioned migrations MAY evolve the contract without changing its authority boundary. The programme MUST NOT introduce a temporary publication-only authority model, require a planned semantic `v1`-to-`v2` migration to add graph authority, or treat an unmerged shadow schema as a production compatibility contract.

**ARCH-010 — Integrated delivery, independent operation.** Publication, serving and governed graph capabilities MUST be implemented and accepted within one target-architecture programme. Dependency order and feature gates MAY control delivery and activation, but MUST NOT create two authority models. Runtime publication correctness MUST remain reconstructable when the graph is unavailable; this degraded-operation property MUST NOT be used to defer the graph identity, event or projector contracts.

**ARCH-011 — Event, operation and consumer-state separation.** A consumer-neutral `LedgerEvent` MUST record an authoritative domain change once. A side-effecting `TargetOperation` MUST record one exact publication-controller command separately. Each projector MUST maintain its own `ProjectionCheckpoint` and `ProjectionFailure` records. An authoritative transaction MUST commit its domain mutation, audit record, ledger events and any required target operations atomically, but MUST NOT create one authoritative outbox row per future read-model consumer.

**ARCH-012 — Event access and replay baseline.** The shared ledger-event envelope MUST contain only non-sensitive routing metadata, including `ledger_seq`, `event_id`, `event_type`, `schema_version`, `aggregate_type`, `aggregate_id`, `aggregate_version`, `recorded_at`, `correlation_id`, `causation_id`, producer version, payload digest or immutable payload reference, and security and retention scope. Payload access MUST be authorised and logged per consumer principal, event type, trust scope and object class. A later consumer MAY replay retained events or initialise from an immutable Authoritative Projection Baseline attested by a ledger record; it MUST NOT fabricate historical events, sequence numbers or recording times.

### Bounded retrieval

**RETR-001 — Named read-only operations.** An agent MUST retrieve governed graph data through allow-listed, read-only operations with limits on relationship type, depth, fan-out, time range, result count, execution time and trust scope. A general write-capable or unbounded generated-query tool MUST NOT be exposed to Hermes.

**RETR-002 — Provenance-rich result.** A retrieval result MUST return stable candidate identifiers, paths, trust state, temporal context, projection metadata and provenance references. Exact source passages, decisions and immutable payloads MUST be hydrated from authoritative records before publication use.

**RETR-003 — No live relationship publication.** A reader-facing related-story, supersession or correction relationship MUST be backed by an effective `RelationAdmissionDecision` and its deterministic Governed Relation view, then bound into the applicable publication bundle. A live graph query MAY suggest a candidate but MUST NOT directly alter a public surface.

### Online serving and semantic projections

**SERV-001 — Canonical serving identity.** Every online article, feed, card or notification record MUST carry stable story and story-version identifiers, its exact surface-payload and publication-bundle digests, visibility and correction state, governed asset references and the applicable `AccessPolicyKey`. The serving platform MUST NOT allocate a competing editorial identity.

**SERV-002 — Versioned access-policy seam.** Free and paid presentation MUST be controlled by an immutable `AccessPolicyRevision` and an authenticated, audited, bitemporal `AccessPolicyAssignment` resolved from the stable `AccessPolicyKey`. An assignment MUST record its non-overlapping effective interval, ledger recording sequence, actor or service principal, authority role, reason, policy digest, prior or superseded assignment and activation decision. Missing, unknown or overlapping assignments MUST fail closed. Changing a free-window, free-item or paid-access rule MUST NOT require rewriting story, story-version, evidence-package, surface-payload or publication-bundle identity. A serving projector or client MUST NOT originate an assignment or grant subscriber entitlement. This seam does not decide the entitlement authority, purchase-proof contract, subscriber-data model or commercial rule.

**SERV-003 — Native Semantic UI projection.** Reader and Web Admin surfaces MUST expose a versioned, machine-readable Semantic UI Projection keyed by canonical identifiers. Its envelope MUST declare schema and producer versions, projection watermark and freshness, canonical content identity, content availability, access decision, loading or resolution progress, public visibility, correction state, operation progress or failure, available actions and stable reason codes. Reader and Admin transition fixtures MUST cover loading, empty, restricted, correction, stale, partial, error, approval and operational states without requiring an agent to infer them from screenshots.

**SERV-004 — Rebuildable serving state.** The online serving store and Newsroom-authored Semantic UI projections MUST be replayable from authoritative bundle, decision, target-operation, acknowledgement, observation, reconciliation and access-policy records. Rebuilding either projection MUST preserve canonical IDs and payload digests and MUST NOT create a new editorial or access-policy decision. Ephemeral device or provider runtime state MAY be composed after projection only where another requirement explicitly identifies it as non-authoritative; it MUST NOT be persisted as projection authority or alter replayable identities.

**SERV-005 — Access-decision projection.** The combined serving and client contract MUST expose a versioned access-decision projection with at least `ALLOWED_FREE`, `ALLOWED_ENTITLED`, `RESTRICTED`, `POLICY_PENDING`, `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING` and `RESOLUTION_ERROR`, together with a stable reason code, evaluated access-policy revision, evaluation time and applicable assignment recording sequence. A material access evaluation MUST remain reconstructable against the exact revision and effective-time decision used. The client MUST render these states explicitly and MUST NOT treat an unresolved or failed decision as free access. This projection does not predetermine whether purchase proof or individual entitlement authority is device-side or server-assisted and does not require a server-held customer profile.

**SERV-006 — Supported client contract.** The serving API and native Semantic UI contract MUST support one Capacitor codebase deployed separately to iPhone, iPad, Android phone and Android tablet. No reader Web client is required for initial activation. Web Admin remains a separate browser surface and MUST consume the same canonical identities and explicit state semantics.

**SERV-007 — Pseudonymous store entitlement.** The initial paid-access authority MUST be a dedicated server-side verifier for Apple App Store and Google Play purchase proof without a first-party Newsroom customer account. It MUST retain only a pseudonymous ecosystem and store-environment subject, product, entitlement status, effective or expiry time, refund or revocation state, verification time and the minimum provider reference or evidence digest required for audit and reconciliation. It MUST NOT retain a name, email address, reader profile or reading history in the entitlement domain. After successful verification it MUST issue a signed, short-lived `AccessGrant`; the content-serving boundary MUST NOT trust a client-side paid flag. Purchase and restore MUST work within the originating store ecosystem. Automatic Apple-to-Google identity linking is out of scope unless a later accepted ADR creates an account-linking boundary.

**SERV-008 — Trial and Free Sample separation.** An introductory free trial MUST be represented as a store-managed, time-bounded Store Entitlement, not as an anonymous device timer or a rule that makes each article free for its first days. Separately designated Free Samples MUST resolve to `ALLOWED_FREE`, remain available without starting a trial or buying a subscription and carry an explicit reader-visible free label. Only an authorised human acting through Web Admin MAY create or revoke a Free Sample Designation; automation MAY propose a candidate but MUST NOT activate or revoke free access. Trial start, expiry, refund or revocation MUST NOT change which items are Free Samples. Changing a Free Sample designation MUST use the access-policy assignment boundary and MUST NOT rewrite story, story-version, surface-payload or publication-bundle identity.

**SERV-009 — Automatic paid preview control.** Every paid article MUST expose its authorised `PreviewExcerpt` by default to a reader without paid entitlement, clearly label the excerpt as a preview and show the restricted continuation state. The unpaid response MUST include non-text content only when its exact governed asset or derivative is `PREVIEW_ALLOWED`; it MUST NOT disclose a restricted object's delivery URL, key or credential. An Inline Paywall Gate MUST follow the authorised preview boundary, or the article header and permitted hero media when the preview is hidden or empty. Opening an article MUST NOT automatically launch a Store or purchase surface; only an explicit reader action on an authorised gate action MAY do so. Only an authorised human acting through Web Admin MAY disable or re-enable preview serving globally through an authenticated and audited Global Preview Control. A control change MUST alter the serving and Semantic UI projection without rewriting story, story-version, surface-payload or publication-bundle identity. Missing, unavailable or indeterminate control state MUST fail closed with previews hidden. A client or generative agent MUST NOT override the control or infer media permission.

**SERV-010 — Store-authoritative commerce display.** The Newsroom server MAY select only the platform Store product identifier and resulting Newsroom access class for an Inline Paywall Gate. The replayable serving and Semantic UI projections MUST contain that selection without commerce terms. The native reader MUST obtain localised price, billing period and eligibility-specific trial or offer terms from the current platform Store product response for that device and Store context, then compose them at render time as explicit ephemeral commerce-metadata and action-availability state. Those terms MUST NOT be hard-coded, authored or inferred by the server, presented from a stale persisted response, written into an authoritative or replayable projection, or treated as entitlement evidence. When current Store Commerce Metadata cannot be obtained, the gate MUST present an honest loading or unavailable state, MUST retain Retry and Restore Purchase actions, MUST disable Subscribe or purchase initiation and MUST NOT show a stale price, billing period, trial or offer claim. Available metadata or offer eligibility MUST NOT produce `ALLOWED_ENTITLED` or an `AccessGrant`; only verified purchase proof resolving to an effective Store Entitlement MAY authorise paid access.

**SERV-011 — Single-primary pre-purchase action.** Before a Store purchase starts, an Inline Paywall Gate MUST expose exactly one Primary Commerce Action. When current Store Commerce Metadata confirms trial eligibility for the active Store context, that action MUST have the semantic identity `START_TRIAL`; otherwise, when metadata is available, it MUST be `SUBSCRIBE`. `RESTORE_PURCHASE` MUST remain a distinct secondary action in either case, and `START_TRIAL` and `SUBSCRIBE` MUST NOT appear as simultaneous competing primary actions. When current metadata cannot be obtained, `RETRY` MUST replace the Primary Commerce Action, `RESTORE_PURCHASE` MUST remain secondary and Subscribe or purchase initiation MUST remain disabled as required by SERV-010. Exact localised copy and visual styling MAY vary without changing these semantic action identities or their hierarchy.

**SERV-012 — Strict entitlement verification barrier.** Invoking `START_TRIAL` or `SUBSCRIBE` MUST put the native reader into the explicit non-entitled purchase-lifecycle state `PURCHASE_IN_PROGRESS` and MUST disable repeated purchase initiation while that operation is active. A native Store success MUST transition the reader to `VERIFYING_ENTITLEMENT` and the access contract to `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING`; it MUST NOT produce `ALLOWED_ENTITLED`, expose restricted content or be treated as entitlement evidence. The article MUST remain restricted until the dedicated server verifier validates the purchase proof, records an effective Store Entitlement and issues a signed `AccessGrant`; only resolution of that grant MAY produce `ALLOWED_ENTITLED`. A Store callback, local receipt, transaction object or client-side success flag alone MUST NOT cross the Entitlement Verification Barrier or optimistically unlock content.

**SERV-013 — Non-error purchase cancellation.** When the native Store reports that the reader cancelled before purchase success, the native reader MUST leave `PURCHASE_IN_PROGRESS`, expose transient `PURCHASE_CANCELLED` as a non-error purchase-lifecycle outcome and keep the article restricted. It MUST NOT enter `VERIFYING_ENTITLEMENT`, submit purchase proof, create or update a Store Entitlement or `AccessGrant`, or present a system-error banner. After the native Store flow closes, the gate MUST return to the current pre-purchase state, re-evaluate the appropriate Primary Commerce Action under SERV-010 and SERV-011, and re-enable that action and `RESTORE_PURCHASE`. Purchase Cancellation MUST remain distinct from purchase failure, refund and Entitlement revocation.

**SERV-014 — Non-terminal verification delay.** When `VERIFYING_ENTITLEMENT` exceeds the configured reader wait limit without either verified entitlement or a terminal verifier result, the native reader MUST expose non-terminal `VERIFICATION_DELAYED`; the access contract MUST remain `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING` and the article MUST remain restricted. `START_TRIAL` and `SUBSCRIBE` MUST remain unavailable so the delay cannot provoke a duplicate purchase. `RETRY_VERIFICATION` MUST be the Primary Commerce Action and `RESTORE_PURCHASE` MUST remain secondary. Retry Verification MUST idempotently resubmit or recheck the same Store transaction or provider proof under a stable verification identity; it MUST NOT initiate a new Store purchase, create duplicate Store Entitlements or issue duplicate `AccessGrant` authority.

**SERV-015 — Single-flight verification recovery.** A Store transaction awaiting verification MUST support both reader-invoked `RETRY_VERIFICATION` and automatic Verification Recovery triggered by network restoration, app foregrounding, a native Store transaction update or an authenticated provider server notification. Every trigger MUST address the same stable verification identity and transaction or provider proof used by SERV-014. Concurrent or closely spaced triggers MUST be coalesced or fenced so that at most one verifier operation for that stable identity is active at a time; later triggers MAY observe or resume that operation but MUST NOT create parallel verification authority. Verification Recovery MUST remain idempotent, MUST NOT invoke a native purchase surface or initiate a new purchase, and MUST produce at most one effective Store Entitlement and `AccessGrant` authority for the verified transaction. Retry backoff, jitter and attempt budget remain activation parameters to be selected separately.

**SERV-016 — Provider-definitive verification failure.** The verifier MUST enter terminal `VERIFICATION_FAILED` only after an authenticated, definitive Store provider verdict establishes that the submitted transaction is invalid or cannot grant the selected Store product and Newsroom access class. Network loss, client or server timeout, rate limiting, provider 5xx or temporary unavailability, a delayed or missing provider notification, an absent response and exhaustion of a retry budget MUST NOT produce Verification Failure; they MUST remain non-terminal `VERIFICATION_DELAYED` with `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING` access and continued eligibility for Verification Recovery. `VERIFICATION_FAILED` MUST remain non-entitled and restricted and MUST NOT create an effective Store Entitlement or `AccessGrant`. Reader-visible reason mapping and recovery actions for the terminal state remain to be selected separately.

**SERV-017 — Restore-first verification-failure recovery.** While the gate is in `VERIFICATION_FAILED`, `RESTORE_PURCHASE` MUST be the Primary Commerce Action and `GET_HELP` MUST be secondary; `START_TRIAL`, `SUBSCRIBE` and `RETRY_VERIFICATION` MUST be unavailable so a definitive failed transaction cannot provoke a duplicate charge or meaningless retry. The automatic context attached to Get Help MUST contain only an opaque diagnostic reference and MUST NOT attach or prepopulate a name, email address, reader identity, reading history or raw provider payload. Any transaction returned by Restore Purchase MUST cross the Entitlement Verification Barrier and follow Verification Recovery where applicable. The gate MUST NOT restore ordinary pre-purchase actions unless a provider-backed restore result definitively confirms that the active Store context has no active or pending entitlement for the selected product; only then MAY it re-evaluate current Store Commerce Metadata under SERV-010 and the Primary Commerce Action under SERV-011.

**SERV-018 — Stable verification-failure reasons.** Every `VERIFICATION_FAILED` result for the submitted transaction MUST map to exactly one stable reader-safe reason: `TRANSACTION_INVALID`, `PRODUCT_MISMATCH`, `STORE_CONTEXT_MISMATCH`, `ENTITLEMENT_INACTIVE` when the submitted transaction is definitively non-effective at verification time, or fallback `PURCHASE_NOT_VERIFIED` for an authenticated definitive rejection that cannot be mapped safely to another class. The reader and Semantic UI contract MUST expose only that stable reason and localised non-technical content derived from it; they MUST NOT expose raw provider codes, messages, payloads or protected verification evidence. Raw provider detail MAY be retained only as access-controlled operational evidence under the applicable minimisation, retention and audit rules. Every reason class MUST preserve the same Restore Purchase and Get Help hierarchy in SERV-017; reason mapping MUST NOT create a purchase or entitlement action. Later expiry, refund or revocation of an already verified Store Entitlement MUST update the entitlement lifecycle and resulting Access Decision under SERV-007 and MUST NOT create `VERIFICATION_FAILED` or invoke Verification Failure Recovery.

**SERV-019 — Privacy-minimised support handoff.** `GET_HELP` MUST open a Newsroom-hosted support form that works without a first-party Newsroom account. Its automatic context MUST contain only the opaque diagnostic reference from SERV-017 and MUST NOT contain a Store receipt, raw provider code or payload, reader identity or reading history. The reader MAY voluntarily supply a reply email address and problem description only after an explicit consent step that identifies the submitted fields. Submission MUST create a Support Case in a role-restricted Web Admin queue accessible only to designated Owner or Support operators; the Support Case MUST NOT become entitlement evidence, create an Entitlement Subject or grant content access. Support Case retention, service level and deletion procedure remain activation decisions to be selected separately.

### Publication state and immutable payloads

**PUBENG-001 — Orthogonal state.** Editorial decision, story-version disposition, public visibility, correction class and per-target delivery MUST be represented as separate state dimensions. The system MUST NOT overload one status field such that a corrected, superseded, withdrawn or partially delivered story loses its other applicable semantics.

**PUBENG-002 — Atomic authorisation and enqueue.** Exact surface-payload and manifest bytes MAY be rendered, validated, atomically installed and left unreferenced before the publication decision. For an authorising outcome, one authoritative transaction MUST commit the decision-free `PublicationBundle` record, its separate authorising `PublicationDecision`, the required audit and ledger events, and one `TargetOperation` per required target and surface payload, or none may become dispatchable. The transaction references the already immutable story version, evidence package and validation results. A refusing decision MUST commit its decision, candidate-manifest digest, reason and audit record but MUST create no authoritative publication-bundle record or target operation.

**PUBENG-003 — Immutable surface payload.** Every editorially meaningful target-specific payload MUST be rendered, validated, content-addressed and included in the publication bundle before dispatch. A target adapter MUST NOT generate, summarise, truncate or otherwise rewrite editorial content.

**PUBENG-004 — Claim-to-surface manifest.** Each publication bundle MUST record which approved claim-evidence record from its Evidence Package occurs in each controlled surface payload, including the applicable field or span and materiality. Direct correction impact MUST be determinable from authoritative records without semantic or graph inference.

**PUBENG-005 — Idempotent public action.** Every target operation MUST have a stable semantic identity. Before external I/O, the controller MUST durably create a fenced attempt with that semantic idempotency key and current authority preconditions; after I/O it MUST record the response or observation separately. Recovery of an unacknowledged attempt MUST use capability-aware correlation lookup or observation before retry. An accepted request followed by a lost response, including on a target without native idempotency or observation, MUST enter an explicit ambiguous-outcome state and require a recorded resolution rather than be blindly retried.

**PUBENG-006 — Dispatch linearisation and cancellation.** A publication-controller worker MUST recheck current bundle authority, pause state, target policy and supersession through the command boundary when conditionally creating a fenced attempt and moving an operation from `PENDING` to `IN_FLIGHT`. A pause, withdrawal, removal or superseding correction committed first MUST prevent stale dispatch. Once an external action is in flight, the system MUST NOT claim that cancellation can prevent every public effect; acknowledgement or ambiguous outcome MUST trigger observation, compensation where possible and reconciliation.

**PUBENG-007 — Truthful publication clocks.** Every public-effect confirmation or observation MUST be an immutable record containing the target, asserted effect time, observation time, method, adapter or observer identity, target-native evidence or digest and verification result. `first_public_effect_at` is a derived value from the earliest valid evidence and MUST preserve the later time and method by which that fact became known. It MUST remain separate from per-attempt `target_acknowledged_at` and `primary_feed_published_at`. The accepted feed policy defines the event that assigns `primary_feed_published_at`; changing that policy MUST NOT falsify or erase an earlier controlled public effect later established through reconciliation.

### Target state, correction and reconciliation

**OPS-001 — Target capability contract.** Every target adapter MUST declare whether it can create, edit, delete, tombstone, query by idempotency key, list items, observe payloads and issue follow-up corrections. Lifecycle behaviour MUST account for these capabilities rather than assume uniform targets.

**OPS-002 — Durable target evidence.** Each target attempt MUST record its immutable command identity, fenced attempt identity, adapter principal, authenticated target context, target-native identifier where available, acknowledgement or failure, relevant target timestamps and raw-response digest. It MUST record the independently observed payload digest and verification result when the target supports observation, otherwise an explicit `NOT_OBSERVABLE` capability limitation. Target responses and observations are untrusted input and MUST be schema-validated and correlated to the exact committed operation and attempt. Invalid, conflicting or unauthenticated evidence MUST remain ambiguous and MUST NOT alter desired editorial state. Delivery success MUST NOT be inferred from worker completion alone.

**OPS-003 — Desired-state reconciliation.** The system MUST periodically compare intended target state with observed state to the extent declared target capabilities permit and classify at least missing, duplicated, stale-version, wrong-payload, unapplied-correction, orphaned, unexpectedly removed and unauthorised-mutation drift. An unobservable class MUST be recorded as `NOT_OBSERVABLE`, never as absence of drift.

**OPS-004 — Correction completion.** A correction, withdrawal or removal workflow is `COMPLETED` only when every required target has reached its valid corrected desired state. An authorised exception or terminal target limitation MUST produce a distinct `PARTIAL` or `TERMINAL_LIMITATION` outcome and MUST NOT satisfy full completion under `LIFE-055`.

**OPS-005 — Graph-independent containment.** Known affected surfaces MUST be correctable, withdrawable or removable from authoritative target mappings while graph services are unavailable. A graph-dependent search for indirect impact MUST hold or report incomplete when its required projection is stale; it MUST NOT claim that the full impact radius is complete.

**OPS-006 — Degraded action matrix.** The system MUST define which discovery, drafting, publication, correction, withdrawal, reassessment and retrieval actions may proceed for each unavailable or stale dependency. Every publication decision MUST carry a dependency-coverage record naming the relationship, source-revision and evidence checks performed, their authoritative cutoff sequence, required projection watermarks and applicable policy rule. A standalone publication MAY proceed without graph availability only when the policy permits authoritative-record checks for that publication class and the coverage record proves them complete. If applicability depends on a projection behind the cutoff or affected by a gap, the action MUST hold rather than infer completeness.

### Conditional local SQLite operating profile

These requirements apply if ADR 0002 is accepted and SQLite is used for the authoritative ledger.

**DBOPS-001 — Enforced command ownership.** A dedicated non-agent service identity MUST own the ledger and authoritative object roots and be the only identity with direct mutation permission. Hermes, generative workers, projectors, publication-controller workers, target adapters and Web Admin clients MUST NOT open or write the ledger directly; the controller records attempts and outcomes through the same command interface. Every mutation MUST enter an authenticated, authorised and audited command interface carrying the caller principal, allow-listed command type, idempotency identity and expected aggregate version or equivalent fencing precondition. A stale command MUST fail rather than overwrite a newer decision.

**DBOPS-002 — Supported SQLite host boundary.** The SQLite file MUST reside on a local filesystem with supported SQLite locking and durability semantics. It MUST NOT be directly opened from NFS, SMB, a cloud-synchronised directory or multiple hosts. Every connection MUST enforce foreign keys. The operating profile MUST define and test WAL and synchronous settings, busy timeout, bounded transaction size and duration, migration ordering and exclusivity, lock admission, checkpoint policy, clean-shutdown checks and scheduled `quick_check` or `integrity_check` behaviour.

**DBOPS-003 — Coordinated recovery point.** A valid off-machine recovery point MUST be published only after the command writer has established one ledger cutoff, created a consistent database snapshot at that cutoff, pinned and copied every governed object reachable from the snapshot, written a content-addressed object manifest and verified every digest. Its descriptor MUST include the cutoff ledger sequence, schema and migration versions, object manifest, snapshot and manifest digests, security and retention scopes and backup format version. Garbage collection MUST preserve objects reachable from every unexpired verified recovery point. An incomplete set MUST never be advertised as restorable.

**DBOPS-004 — Secure deletion-aware restore.** Ledger snapshots, manifests and governed objects MUST use authenticated encryption in transit and at rest. Decryption keys MUST be separated from backup storage and agent-accessible configuration; restore authority MUST be restricted, logged and covered by rotation, recovery and revocation procedures. Publication credentials MUST NOT enter the backup set. An authoritative deletion and tombstone journal MUST be protected in a separate recovery domain. Restored data MUST remain quarantined until every later applicable deletion has been replayed, covered objects and derivatives are verified absent, database and object integrity pass and the recovery point is approved for projections or publication. Backup expiry and cryptographic erasure MUST follow the same deletion scope.

**DBOPS-005 — Measurable operating envelope.** Before production activation, the owner MUST approve numeric limits and alert thresholds for at least p95 and p99 command-transaction latency, maximum lock wait and transaction duration, write rate and contention, target-operation and projection backlog, database and bounded BLOB size, disk headroom, backup freshness, recovery point objective, recovery time objective, restore window and restore-drill frequency. Exceeding an approved safety threshold persistently MUST block unsafe scale-up and trigger the ADR 0002 migration review.

## Acceptance criteria

1. A crash at each governed-object installation boundary never leaves an accepted ledger reference to missing or unverified bytes; orphaned objects remain safely collectable.
2. Deleting graph, vector and full-text projections and replaying authoritative records restores structurally identical canonical entities, Governed Claims, Governed Relations and provenance without rerunning extraction, while golden retrieval metrics remain within predeclared tolerances.
3. A failed projection event followed by a later available event exposes a gap and cannot advance the contiguous watermark past the failure.
4. A high-confidence malicious proposal and a rights-unknown observed source cannot enter an approved Evidence Package.
5. An authorised standalone bundle remains publishable during graph outage only with a complete dependency-coverage record at the required cutoff; a story whose relationship applicability depends on a stale or gapped projection holds.
6. For an authorising outcome, the decision-free publication-bundle record, separate decision, audit and ledger events and all required target operations are either committed together or absent together; staged bytes alone are never dispatchable and a refusing decision creates neither a bundle record nor an operation.
7. A target with native idempotency, correlation or observation that accepts an action and loses the response produces one public item after recovery, not a blind duplicate; an unobservable target remains explicitly ambiguous until a governed resolution.
8. A pause or superseding correction committed before `PENDING` becomes `IN_FLIGHT` prevents stale dispatch; if committed after that boundary, any resulting effect is detected and reconciled or compensated according to target capability.
9. Invalidating one claim identifies every directly affected controlled surface from the publication manifest while graph services are offline.
10. Reconciliation detects each observable required drift class and records remediation; unsupported observation is explicitly classified and cannot be presented as clean state.
11. Expiring or deleting a protected object purges every derivative covered by the recorded deletion scope, retains only permitted audit or tombstone evidence, and a clean rebuild does not restore prohibited material.
12. A restored ledger and object store remain quarantined until later deletions are replayed and database, object, audit and deletion-scope verification pass; projection and publication cannot resume earlier.
13. A fresh installation creates canonical production schema v1 and can run an end-to-end source-observation, entity-resolution, claim-and-relation proposal, admission, graph-projection, publication-bundle, target-dispatch and serving-projection flow without applying a temporary-stage migration.
14. Publication, serving, governed-knowledge and operations projectors consume the same stable identifiers and ordered ledger events; target adapters consume only committed target operations. Deleting and rebuilding a projection does not rewrite authoritative identities or require adapter-specific remapping.
15. Changing the active free/paid assignment preserves story, story-version, surface-payload and publication-bundle identities, resolves the exact bitemporal policy revision and produces an explicit restricted, free, entitled, pending or error access decision.
16. Reader and Admin Semantic UI projections expose the same canonical IDs, schema and producer versions, projection watermark and explicit loading, access, correction, approval, partial and error states before and after a clean rebuild.
17. Filesystem permissions prevent Hermes, workers, projectors and clients from mutating the ledger or authoritative object roots; authenticated commands with stale aggregate versions fail closed, and the SQLite file cannot be opened from a network or cloud-synchronised filesystem.
18. A recovery drill restores one verified encrypted database-and-object recovery point within the approved objectives, preserves every object pinned by retained recovery points, excludes publication credentials and proves that a deletion effective before or after the snapshot cannot be resurrected.
19. A later authorised consumer can rebuild from retained events or a complete verified Authoritative Projection Baseline and later events without fabricated sequence or timestamps; an incomplete baseline cannot advance its checkpoint.
20. A public effect observed after the fact preserves both the asserted effect time and the later observation record while deriving `first_public_effect_at`; feed order and acknowledgement history remain unchanged.
21. iPhone, iPad, Android phone and Android tablet clients consume the same serving and Semantic UI contracts and fail closed rather than showing free content when access resolution is pending or failed.
22. A valid store purchase and restore produces a short-lived signed Access Grant within its originating ecosystem without creating a Newsroom account; refund or revocation removes future paid grants, and an Apple entitlement is not silently linked to an Android subject.
23. An authorised human can designate and visibly label a Free Sample through Web Admin, while an automated suggestion cannot activate it; a reader with no trial or subscription can open it, and starting or expiring a store-managed trial neither relabels it nor turns article age into an access rule.
24. A paid article exposes its exact authorised Preview Excerpt by default and includes only `PREVIEW_ALLOWED` non-text assets without leaking restricted delivery references; its Inline Paywall Gate follows the resulting boundary without automatically opening a purchase surface. An authenticated global disable hides every paid preview without changing editorial identity, an unauthorised or client-side re-enable fails, and the audited owner control can restore preview serving.
25. The Inline Paywall Gate displays localised price, billing period and eligibility-specific trial or offer terms only from current platform Store Commerce Metadata for the server-selected product, composed as ephemeral native-client state rather than a replayable Newsroom projection. If that metadata is unavailable, the gate presents no stale commerce claim, retains Retry and Restore Purchase and disables Subscribe or purchase initiation. Injecting available metadata or trial eligibility without verified purchase proof leaves access restricted and produces neither `ALLOWED_ENTITLED` nor an `AccessGrant`; verified proof resolving to an effective Store Entitlement is required.
26. With current Store metadata available, the gate exposes one primary `START_TRIAL` action only for a Store context confirmed as trial-eligible and one primary `SUBSCRIBE` action otherwise; `RESTORE_PURCHASE` remains secondary and the two commerce actions never compete as simultaneous primaries. With metadata unavailable, `RETRY` is the only primary action, Restore Purchase remains secondary and purchase initiation is disabled.
27. Invoking `START_TRIAL` or `SUBSCRIBE` produces `PURCHASE_IN_PROGRESS` and rejects repeated purchase initiation. Injecting a native Store success, local receipt or transaction object without successful server verification produces `VERIFYING_ENTITLEMENT` and `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING`, leaves the article restricted and cannot produce `ALLOWED_ENTITLED`; only an effective Store Entitlement and signed `AccessGrant` unlock paid content.
28. Injecting a reader-originated native Store cancellation produces transient non-error `PURCHASE_CANCELLED`, leaves the article restricted, starts no entitlement verification, creates no Store Entitlement or `AccessGrant`, presents no system-error banner and returns to the current pre-purchase gate with its appropriate Primary Commerce Action and `RESTORE_PURCHASE` re-enabled.
29. Holding verification without a terminal result beyond the configured reader wait limit produces non-terminal `VERIFICATION_DELAYED`, keeps `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING` and the article restricted, removes Start Trial and Subscribe, and presents primary `RETRY_VERIFICATION` with secondary `RESTORE_PURCHASE`. Repeated retries use the same stable verification identity and Store transaction, create no new purchase or duplicate entitlement authority, and can still converge on one valid `AccessGrant` after successful verification.
30. Firing reader retry, network restoration, app foregrounding, native transaction update and authenticated provider-notification triggers concurrently for one delayed Store transaction produces at most one active verifier operation under the same stable verification identity, opens no purchase surface and yields at most one effective Store Entitlement and `AccessGrant` authority.
31. An authenticated, definitive provider rejection of a transaction or its applicability to the selected product and access class produces terminal `VERIFICATION_FAILED`, remains restricted and creates no entitlement authority. Injecting network loss, timeout, throttling, provider 5xx or unavailability, delayed notification, absent response or exhausted retry budget instead preserves `VERIFICATION_DELAYED`, `ENTITLEMENT_PENDING` and Verification Recovery eligibility.
32. In `VERIFICATION_FAILED`, the gate exposes primary `RESTORE_PURCHASE` and secondary `GET_HELP`, exposes none of Start Trial, Subscribe or Retry Verification, and attaches only an opaque diagnostic reference to Get Help. A restore result containing an active or pending transaction remains inside entitlement verification; only a provider-backed result confirming neither exists can rebuild the ordinary pre-purchase gate.
33. Each provider-definitive Verification Failure for the submitted transaction maps to exactly one of `TRANSACTION_INVALID`, `PRODUCT_MISMATCH`, `STORE_CONTEXT_MISMATCH`, `ENTITLEMENT_INACTIVE` at verification time or `PURCHASE_NOT_VERIFIED`; the reader receives only the stable reason and localised non-technical content, while raw provider detail remains absent from the reader contract and protected as operational evidence. Every reason retains the same Restore Purchase and Get Help actions. Injecting later expiry, refund or revocation for an already verified Store Entitlement updates entitlement and access state without producing `VERIFICATION_FAILED` or Restore-first failure recovery.
34. Get Help opens a Newsroom-hosted form containing only the opaque diagnostic reference until the reader explicitly consents to submit an optional reply address and description. The resulting Support Case contains no receipt, raw provider detail or reading history, appears only in the role-restricted Web Admin queue, creates no Newsroom account or entitlement authority and grants no content access.

## Non-goals

This specification does not select SQLite, PostgreSQL, Neo4j, Graphiti, LadybugDB, a cloud provider, a queue implementation or a target SDK. It does not authorise a temporary graph-less authority model, Discord or OpenClaw as target dependencies, or the use of graph retrieval as publication evidence without the existing editorial gates. A plan may sequence implementation dependencies, but it may not reinterpret that sequence as separate target architectures.
